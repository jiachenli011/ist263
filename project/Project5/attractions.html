Shanghai's Must-See Attractions

This page highlights must-see attractions in Shanghai and suggests travel routes. Detailed introductions and images help visitors plan trips, discovering historical buildings, modern landmarks, and natural scenery.

The Bund: A Fusion of History and Modernity

The Bund showcases Shanghai’s mix of past and present, with historical Western-style buildings alongside views of the Lujiazui skyline across the river. It’s a popular photo spot, especially at dusk.

Yu Garden and City God Temple

Yu Garden, a classical garden from the Ming Dynasty, offers a peaceful retreat, while the nearby City God Temple provides a glimpse into Taoist beliefs and Shanghai’s cultural roots.

Tianzifang: Art and Creativity Hub

Tianzifang, a former Shikumen neighborhood, is now a creative paradise, with galleries, studios, and cafes lining the alleyways, making it an ideal spot to experience local culture and creativity.

Lujiazui: The Modern Skyline

Famous for skyscrapers like the Oriental Pearl Tower and Shanghai Tower, Lujiazui offers observation decks for panoramic city views. Binjiang Park is a relaxing spot to admire both historical and modern Shanghai.

Chongming Island: Natural and Ecological Haven

Chongming Island, with its wetlands and forests, is ideal for nature lovers. The island offers birdwatching, cycling routes, and farm stays, allowing visitors to enjoy Shanghai’s natural beauty at a slower pace.

Recommended Routes

  • Cultural Classic One-Day Tour: The Bund, Yu Garden, City God Temple, and Tianzifang.
  • Nature and City Combo: Chongming Island and Lujiazui night view.
  • Two-Day History and Modernity: Day 1 - The Bund, Yu Garden, Tianzifang; Day 2 - Lujiazui and Binjiang Park.
