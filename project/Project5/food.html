Savoring Shanghai: A Journey Through Flavors and Markets

This page takes visitors on a culinary journey through Shanghai’s vibrant food culture and markets. Visitors will get a comprehensive understanding of Shanghai’s cuisine and cultural roots through local dishes and famous markets.

Shanghai’s Culinary Characteristics

Shanghai’s “Benbang” cuisine is known for its rich, savory flavors. Classic dishes include Red-Braised Pork, Drunken Dishes, and Oil-Burst Shrimp. These dishes highlight the city’s blend of history and culinary innovation.

Traditional Snacks

Iconic snacks like Nanxiang Xiaolongbao, Scallion Oil Noodles, and Shengjianbao are must-tries for visitors. Other traditional snacks include Tiaotou Cake, Haitang Cake, and Crab Roe Soup Dumplings, all offering a taste of old Shanghai.

Market Culture in Shanghai

Visiting markets is an essential part of experiencing Shanghai’s culture. Chenghuang Temple Market and Yuyuan Snack Plaza offer a variety of snacks and souvenirs. Qibao Ancient Town Market provides a nostalgic look at Shanghai’s past.

The Integration of International Cuisine

Shanghai has embraced global cuisines, with French, Italian, and Japanese foods available citywide. Xintiandi and Global Harbor are hubs for international dining, showcasing Shanghai’s global appeal.

Travel Tips

For an authentic experience, visitors are encouraged to visit traditional wet markets in the morning and explore food stalls in busy streets or shopping malls at any time.
